Congratulations!

You’re getting married! Or at least thinking about it?

I put together this website to help you plan your celebration of Holy Matrimony, aka your Catholic wedding. There are a couple of books available, and numerous blog posts online with specific pieces of advice and background, but none of these are as comprehensive or clear as I think they could be. After finding myself sharing the same information with multiple couples, I decided to put it all together in one place. Enjoy!

A word of advice

This might seem obvious, but I highly encourage you to pray with the material in these pages! This is a wonderful opportunity for you to craft a profound moment of prayer that will propel you into your married life. It is also a wonderful opportunity to share a spirit of invitation and welcome with the gathered assembly, many of whom may not be regular participants in Catholic liturgy.

Second, it might be helpful to consider how your choice of a Catholic wedding is distinctive in today’s society. You will not be crafting a ceremony from scratch because, ultimately, your wedding is not about you. (I know, crazy, right?!?) It is about the gift of love you and your spouse have received from God, and the ways in which you are being called to share that love in community. This is an experience that Christians have had and considered sacred since the earliest days of the church. That tradition is now being handed on to you in the form of a liturgy that has its own integrity and meaning. You will be presented with a variety of options in the following pages. I suggest you think of these not only as options for what you want, but as alternative ways of communicating a theology of marriage that is true to Christian belief and to your own experience.

Blessing your engagement

If you have not already, you might consider celebrating the Blessing of an Engaged Couple to ask for God’s blessing, and the blessing of your loved ones, to support and guide you as you prepare to be married. You can celebrate this brief prayer service at home or at church (although not during Mass), and with a priest, deacon, or even one of your parents or another trusted person presiding. You can also include it as part of a gathering of your two families. This blessing includes a blessing of your engagement rings.
